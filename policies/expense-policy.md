# Expense Reimbursement Policy

## Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for business expense reimbursement to ensure appropriate use of company funds while supporting business objectives.

## General Principles

### Reimbursable Expenses

Expenses must be:
- **Business-related**: Directly related to company business
- **Reasonable**: Appropriate in amount and nature
- **Necessary**: Required for business purposes
- **Documented**: Supported by proper receipts and documentation

### Employee Responsibility

Employees are expected to:
- Exercise good judgment in spending company money
- Seek the most economical options while meeting business needs
- Obtain proper approvals before incurring expenses
- Submit expense reports promptly with required documentation

## Travel Expenses

### Transportation

**Air Travel**
- Economy class for domestic flights under 4 hours
- Business class allowed for international flights over 6 hours
- Book flights at least 14 days in advance when possible
- Use company-preferred travel agencies when available

**Ground Transportation**
- Rental cars: Mid-size or smaller unless business needs require larger
- Taxi/Rideshare: Reasonable for business purposes
- Mileage: $0.65 per mile for personal vehicle use
- Parking: Actual costs with receipts

**Public Transportation**
- Train, bus, subway fares fully reimbursable
- First-class train travel allowed for trips over 3 hours

### Lodging

**Hotel Accommodations**
- Standard business-class hotels
- Maximum $200 per night in major cities, $150 in other locations
- Extended stay discounts should be utilized for trips over 7 days
- Personal expenses (movies, minibar, spa) not reimbursable

**Alternative Lodging**
- Airbnb/VRBO allowed with pre-approval
- Must be comparable or less expensive than hotel options
- Business-appropriate locations only

### Meals and Entertainment

**Meal Allowances**
- **Breakfast**: $15 per day
- **Lunch**: $25 per day  
- **Dinner**: $40 per day
- **International**: 150% of domestic rates

**Business Meals**
- Client entertainment meals fully reimbursable with business purpose
- Alcohol allowed in moderation for business meals
- Tip: 15-20% standard, included in meal limits

**Team Meals**
- Department team meals allowed monthly (manager approval)
- Company events and celebrations covered separately
- Alcohol allowed for team building events

## Office and Equipment Expenses

### Office Supplies

**Standard Supplies**
- Pens, paper, notebooks, folders
- Computer accessories (mouse, keyboard, monitor)
- Desk organizers and basic furniture
- Maximum $100 per month without pre-approval

**Technology Equipment**
- Software licenses and subscriptions
- Computer hardware upgrades (with IT approval)
- Mobile devices and accessories
- Requires manager and IT approval

### Professional Development

**Training and Education**
- Job-related courses, certifications, conferences
- Maximum $2,000 per employee per year
- Requires manager approval and business justification
- Company may require commitment period for expensive training

**Books and Publications**
- Professional books, magazines, online subscriptions
- Maximum $500 per year per employee
- Must be job-related

## Communication Expenses

### Phone and Internet

**Mobile Phone**
- $50 monthly stipend for employees using personal phones for business
- Company phones provided for roles requiring extensive travel
- International roaming charges require pre-approval

**Internet Service**
- Home internet upgrade reimbursement for remote workers
- Maximum $50 per month
- Requires documentation of business need

## Expense Reporting Process

### Submission Requirements

**Timing**
- Submit expense reports within 30 days of expense
- Monthly submission recommended
- Late submissions may be denied

**Documentation**
- Original receipts for all expenses over $25
- Credit card statements not acceptable as sole documentation
- Business purpose and attendees must be noted

**Approval Workflow**
1. Employee submits expense report with documentation
2. Direct manager reviews and approves
3. Finance department processes payment
4. Reimbursement via direct deposit within 10 business days

### Required Information

For each expense, provide:
- Date of expense
- Amount and currency
- Business purpose
- Attendees (for meals and entertainment)
- Client/project code (if applicable)

## Non-Reimbursable Expenses

### Personal Expenses

- Personal meals when not traveling
- Personal entertainment and recreation
- Spouse/family travel expenses (unless approved business purpose)
- Personal shopping and souvenirs
- Traffic violations and parking tickets

### Prohibited Expenses

- Alcoholic beverages (except business meals)
- Adult entertainment
- Personal grooming services
- Excessive or luxury items
- Cash advances or loans

## Credit Cards and Payment Methods

### Company Credit Cards

**Eligibility**
- Employees with regular travel requirements
- Manager-level positions
- Requires credit check and approval

**Usage Guidelines**
- Business expenses only
- Monthly reconciliation required
- Personal use strictly prohibited

### Personal Payment

- Employees may pay personally and seek reimbursement
- Corporate rates may not be available
- Reimbursement processed after approval

## International Travel

### Additional Requirements

- Passport and visa expenses reimbursable for business travel
- Currency exchange at reasonable rates
- International phone/data plans pre-approved
- Medical insurance coverage verified

### Per Diem Rates

- Follow U.S. State Department per diem rates by location
- Actual expense method also acceptable with receipts
- Local customs and tipping practices considered

## Policy Violations

### Consequences

- Denial of reimbursement
- Repayment of inappropriate expenses
- Disciplinary action
- Removal of credit card privileges
- Termination for serious violations

### Appeals Process

Employees may appeal denied expenses through:
1. Discussion with direct manager
2. HR department review
3. Finance department final decision
