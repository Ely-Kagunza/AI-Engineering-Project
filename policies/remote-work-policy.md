# Remote Work Policy

## Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for remote work arrangements to maintain productivity, collaboration, and work-life balance while supporting business objectives.

## Eligibility

### Employee Requirements

To be eligible for remote work, employees must:
- Complete 6 months of employment with satisfactory performance
- Demonstrate self-motivation and time management skills
- Have a suitable home office environment
- Maintain reliable internet connection (minimum 25 Mbps download/upload)

### Position Requirements

Remote work is available for positions that:
- Do not require physical presence for core job functions
- Can be performed effectively with available technology
- Allow for adequate supervision and collaboration

## Remote Work Options

### Hybrid Schedule

- Up to 3 days per week remote work
- Minimum 2 days per week in office (typically Tuesday and Thursday)
- Core collaboration hours: 10 AM - 3 PM (must be available)

### Full Remote

- Available for specific roles and exceptional circumstances
- Requires director-level approval
- Subject to quarterly performance reviews

### Temporary Remote

- Available for short-term needs (illness, family emergencies, weather)
- Requires manager approval
- Duration typically limited to 2 weeks

## Home Office Requirements

### Workspace Setup

- Dedicated, quiet workspace free from distractions
- Ergonomic desk and chair setup
- Adequate lighting and ventilation
- Professional background for video calls

### Technology Requirements

- Company-provided laptop and necessary software
- Reliable high-speed internet connection
- Webcam and headset for video conferences
- Secure Wi-Fi network (no public networks for work)

### Security Measures

- Use of company VPN for all work activities
- Secure storage of company documents and equipment
- Compliance with data protection policies
- Regular software updates and security patches

## Communication and Collaboration

### Availability Expectations

- Maintain regular business hours (9 AM - 5 PM local time)
- Respond to messages within 2 hours during business hours
- Participate in scheduled meetings and video conferences
- Update calendar with availability and time zone if different

### Communication Tools

- **Slack**: Daily team communication and quick updates
- **Zoom**: Video conferences and team meetings
- **Email**: Formal communications and external correspondence
- **Project Management Tools**: Task tracking and project updates

### Meeting Guidelines

- Camera on for all team meetings
- Professional appearance and background
- Mute when not speaking
- Test technology before important meetings

## Performance and Accountability

### Performance Standards

Remote employees are held to the same performance standards as in-office employees, including:
- Meeting deadlines and deliverables
- Quality of work output
- Participation in team activities
- Professional development goals

### Monitoring and Check-ins

- Weekly one-on-one meetings with direct supervisor
- Monthly team meetings (may be in-person)
- Quarterly performance reviews
- Annual remote work agreement renewal

## Expenses and Equipment

### Company-Provided Equipment

- Laptop computer and necessary software licenses
- Monitor, keyboard, and mouse (upon request)
- Headset and webcam
- Mobile phone stipend ($50/month for eligible employees)

### Reimbursable Expenses

- Internet service upgrade (up to $50/month)
- Ergonomic office furniture (up to $500 annually)
- Office supplies (up to $25/month)
- Co-working space membership (with pre-approval)

### Non-Reimbursable Expenses

- Utilities (electricity, heating, cooling)
- Home office rent or mortgage
- Personal phone service
- Coffee and meals

## Travel and In-Person Requirements

### Mandatory In-Person Events

- Quarterly all-hands meetings
- Annual company retreat
- Team building activities (as scheduled)
- Client meetings (when required)

### Travel Expectations

- Remote employees may be required to travel to office location
- Travel expenses covered by company
- Advance notice provided (minimum 2 weeks)

## Policy Violations and Termination

### Performance Issues

Failure to meet remote work standards may result in:
- Return to full-time office work
- Performance improvement plan
- Disciplinary action up to termination

### Policy Violations

Violations of remote work policy include:
- Unauthorized location changes
- Inadequate availability during business hours
- Security breaches or policy non-compliance
- Misuse of company equipment or resources

## Agreement and Approval Process

1. Employee submits remote work request form
2. Manager evaluates request and employee eligibility
3. HR reviews and approves arrangement
4. Employee signs remote work agreement
5. 30-day trial period with evaluation
6. Annual renewal required
